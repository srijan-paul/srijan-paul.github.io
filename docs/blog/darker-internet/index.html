The internet is slipping out of our reach

Mar 4
miscellaneous technology

How often do you append "reddit" or "wiki" to your Google search queries?
Why seek out human discourse on specific websites, when search engines were invented to do it for you?

If you asked Google for workout routines a decade ago, you would find workout routines. If you ask Google for a workout routines today, you'll find: paid search results, ads, third party cookies, trackers, newsletter prompts, notification access requests, more ads, and, if you're lucky – a workout routine1. No matter what you search for, SEO hacked content farms will blot out the sun.2

This is in stark contrast with the old internet – a small web where sites run by individuals were easier to find, and communities developed organically across multiple forums. Gothic mods and Starcraft fanfics weren't subreddits; and personal websites didn't live on substack or medium.3

In the race to commoditize our attention, everything has moved to select few apps: Videos on YT, Images on Instagram, rants on Twitter, questions on Quora, and newsletters on Substack. Everything outside this bubble is SEO spam by content farms fighting for their place on your search page.

But you know what's worse than SEO spam? AI generated SEO spam.

In the underbelly of AI enabled products, there are services that generate thousands of articles, post them on multiple platforms, then create fake social media profiles to market them.
